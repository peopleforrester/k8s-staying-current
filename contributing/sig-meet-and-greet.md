# SIG Meet & Greet at KubeCon

The SIG Meet & Greet is one of the most valuable sessions at every KubeCon — and one of the most overlooked.

---

## What It Is

A casual lunch session in the **Project Pavilion** where SIG leads and active contributors set up tables for their SIGs. You walk up, sit down, and talk to the people who actually build and maintain Kubernetes.

No presentation. No slides. Just conversation.

---

## Why You Should Go

- **Meet the maintainers** — Put faces to the names you see on GitHub and Slack.
- **Ask questions directly** — "How do I get started with SIG Network?" is a perfectly valid opening.
- **Learn what each SIG needs** — Maintainers will tell you exactly what they need help with — and it's often not code.
- **Find your SIG** — Not sure which SIG aligns with your interests? This is the fastest way to find out.

---

## How It Works

1. **Check the schedule** — The Meet & Greet is listed on the KubeCon schedule (sched.com). Time and exact location vary by event.
2. **Show up** — It's during a lunch period. Grab food, find the SIG tables.
3. **Walk up to a table** — Look for the SIG name on a sign or banner. Sit down.
4. **Introduce yourself** — "Hi, I'm [name], I'm interested in [area], and I want to get involved" is all you need.
5. **Listen** — SIG leads often share what they're working on and where they need help.
6. **Follow up on Slack** — After the session, join the SIG's Slack channel and reference the conversation.

---

## Tips

- **Don't be intimidated.** SIG leads at the Meet & Greet are there specifically to meet new contributors. They want you to show up.
- **Have a question ready.** Even something simple like "What's the biggest challenge for your SIG right now?" starts a great conversation.
- **Visit multiple tables.** You have the whole lunch period. Try 2–3 SIGs.
- **Take notes.** Write down names, Slack handles, and any issues or repos they mention. Follow up within 48 hours.
- **Not a coder?** SIGs need help with documentation, testing, issue triage, and community management. Say so — they'll point you in the right direction.
