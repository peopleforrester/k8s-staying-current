# Key Kubernetes Slack Channels

Kubernetes Slack ([slack.k8s.io](https://slack.k8s.io)) is the primary real-time communication platform for the Kubernetes community, with 300,000+ members.

---

## Getting Started

1. **Sign up:** [slack.k8s.io](https://slack.k8s.io)
2. **Join your role-specific channels** (see below)
3. **Lurk for a week** to get the rhythm, then start participating

---

## Essential Channels (Everyone)

| Channel | What It's For |
|---------|--------------|
| `#kubernetes-users` | General questions and discussion |
| `#announcements` | Official announcements (low traffic, high signal) |
| `#kubernetes-dev` | Cross-SIG development discussions |
| `#kubernetes-contributors` | Contributor coordination |

## The 2025 Slack Scare

In June 2025, Salesforce threatened to downgrade the Kubernetes Slack workspace. The community mobilized, and the downgrade was cancelled. Slack remains active and is still the primary real-time channel.

However, the dependency on Salesforce's platform policies is a known concern. No migration has been announced, but the conversation about alternatives is ongoing. For now: **stay on Slack.**

## Slack Tips

- **Don't try to read everything.** Pick 2–3 channels relevant to your role and scan them weekly.
- **Use threads.** Keep conversations organized — reply in threads, not in the main channel.
- **Search before asking.** Many common questions have been answered before.
- **Be patient.** Contributors are volunteers across every timezone.
- **Star important channels** so they stay visible in your sidebar.
- **Set notifications wisely** — most SIG channels are high-volume. Use "mentions and keywords" notification settings rather than "all messages."
